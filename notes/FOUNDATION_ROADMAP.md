# Foundation roadmap — turning the corpus into a prioritized target map

**Correction to an earlier claim.** I previously called the 486-claim corpus "thin"
because few claims are *directly* auto-provable. That framing was wrong. The corpus is
not thin as knowledge — it is a high-quality **priority map for foundation building**.
When each formalizable claim is tagged by the *missing Lean object* it depends on, the
corpus tells us exactly which few foundations to build, and in what order, to unlock the
most ECDLP attack/structure claims at once.

This is the right use of "all the collected data": not as a bag of leaf targets, but as
the **dependency graph that schedules the deep work**.

## What this schedules

**Near term (days — breadth, no new foundations):** harvest the "formalizable now" layer.
This is the server-daemon + frontier-generator backlog: generic group/arithmetic facts,
order/Lagrange, secp256k1 numeric facts, the torsion-lattice. Mechanically gradable and
mostly tactic-closable.

**Medium term (weeks — isogeny/endomorphism, rank 5):** parts already exist in Mathlib
(`WeierstrassCurve`, division polynomials we already use). Formalizing the GLV `λ`-action
(`glv-*` claims, 10) and the trace/anomalous *scope* facts is the most tractable deep
direction and connects to proved work (`CubeRoot.lean`, `TraceOfFrobenius.lean`).

**The summit (months — rank 1, the Weil pairing):** highest leverage (49 claims) but a
multi-month, research-grade Mathlib contribution: it needs divisors on curves / a function
field / Miller's algorithm before `eₙ` can even be *stated*. This is why those claims
"can't be auto-closed" today — not because tactics fail, but because **the object `eₙ`
does not exist in Lean, so the statement cannot be written at all.** Building it is the
unlock; everything downstream (MOV, Frey–Rück) follows.

## Why "can't auto-close" ≠ "tactics too weak"

A proof tactic searches for a *proof of a stated goal*. If the goal mentions `eₙ` and `eₙ`
is undefined, there is no goal to search — you cannot type the theorem. So the deep rungs
are blocked at the **definition** layer, not the **proof** layer. The corpus's value is
precisely that it ranks *which definitions to build first* by how many real claims each
one releases.
