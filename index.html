What it is

KeyAI turns a body of mathematics into a verified, machine-navigable substrate: a graph of theorems that are not merely asserted but proved, re-checkable from source, pinned to an exact toolchain. On top of it runs an engine that proposes, proves, and promotes new results — with the kernel as the gate. Nothing enters the base unless Lean accepts it with no sorry and no added axioms.

The engine

Decompose→ Prove→ Verify · Lean kernel→ Promote

A three-party harness separates ideas from truth: language models decompose problems and draft proofs; a warm Lean toolchain verifies each candidate in seconds; only kernel-accepted results are promoted into the base through review. The generator only proposes statements — it never asserts a proof.

The current asset — secp256k1 & the ECDLP

What is verified

The arithmetic of the secp256k1 curve and generic-hardness statements for the elliptic-curve discrete-log problem: primality of the field and group order, torsion structure, the GLV endomorphism, division-polynomial degrees and coprimality, Baby-step/Giant-step and Pollard-ρ bounds, and more.

Honest by construction

This is not an attack. The discrete-log hardness (~2128) is unchanged; the value is the verified asset and the reusable engine. Negative and no-go results are first-class. We map the frontier honestly — the kernel forbids wishful thinking.

Roadmap — one engine, widening scope

  1. secp256k1 / ECDLP. The proving ground: a self-contained verified corpus and a working autonomous pipeline.
  2. General elliptic-curve arithmetic → upstream Mathlib. The same machinery produces reusable, curve-agnostic contributions — e.g. the open Mathlib TODO on elliptic divisibility sequences — that benefit the whole library.
  3. BSD-adjacent infrastructure. The north star: formalize the components around elliptic curves over ℚ — heights, ranks, L-functions — as a long-horizon program, not a bet on solving the unsolvable.

Principles

The kernel is the judge

Correctness is decided by Lean, not by a model's confidence. A green build means every built theorem is fully proved.

Honesty over hype

We report what is verified, what is assumed, and what is open — with counts that don't drift and trust boundaries stated in full.

Reproducible

Mathlib is pinned; every result re-checks from source on the exact toolchain. No hidden state, no un-audited axioms.

Reusable engine

The ontology and target layers are replaceable, so the generator + prover generalize beyond any single domain.
